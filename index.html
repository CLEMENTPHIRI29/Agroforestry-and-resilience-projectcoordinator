I am a Programmes Manager and Irrigation Engineer with nine years of progressive experience leading large-scale, donor-funded agroforestry, resilience, and livelihoods programmes in Malawi. My BSc in Irrigation Engineering from the University of Malawi gives me a technical foundation — soil-water dynamics, watershed management, irrigation system design — that most development professionals cannot offer.

From 2016 to 2022 I served as Programmes Manager at the Foundation for Irrigation and Sustainable Development (FISD), where I led the full 84-month lifecycle of a WFP-funded integrated resilience programme. I took it from concept note to donor award, delivered across 21 field sites, managed a 17-person multidisciplinary team, and maintained zero major audit findings across every donor reporting cycle.

I have operated in humanitarian emergency contexts — including the Cyclone Idai response in 2019 — where I maintained programme delivery while adjusting priorities in real time. I have deep, pre-existing working relationships with District Agriculture, Forestry, and Community Development offices across the Chikwawa–Mwanza corridor: the exact project geography for AGREE Malawi.

Since 2022, I have been a Technical Engineer at Davis & Shirtliff, Malawi's leading water and energy solutions company, deepening my applied irrigation and solar energy expertise. I am now ready to bring everything — the programme management, the technical depth, the donor compliance experience, and the community relationships — to Save the Children's AGREE Malawi project.

"I do not just manage projects — I deliver measurable improvements in farmer livelihoods that directly protect children."
